**Timeline:** 4/5 → 8/5 (5 working days)

## Day 1 - 4/5: ECS Learning Amazon EBS

**Work completed:** I studied Amazon Elastic Block Store (Amazon EBS) and learned about the role of block storage for Amazon EC2 instances. I explored different types of EBS volumes, their storage scalability, and how EBS volumes operate within an Availability Zone. In addition, I reviewed common use cases for Amazon EBS in cloud environments.

**Result and lesson:** I gained an understanding of the role of Amazon EBS in providing persistent storage for EC2 instances and learned how to choose the appropriate storage type for different workloads.

## Day 2 - 5/5: Creating and Attaching EBS Volumes

**Work completed:** I practiced creating a new EBS volume, attaching it to an EC2 instance, verifying the available storage capacity on the server, and detaching the volume after completing the exercise. I also learned the recommended procedures for safely managing storage volumes to prevent data loss.

**Result and lesson:**  I understood the process of managing Amazon EBS volumes and learned how to properly attach and detach storage devices in AWS.

## Day 3 - 6/5: Practicing Amazon EBS Snapshots

**Work completed:**  I studied the Snapshot feature of Amazon EBS and practiced creating backups from an active EBS volume. I also explored the process of restoring data from snapshots and learned when snapshots should be used for backup and disaster recovery purposes.

**Result and lesson:** I understood the importance of Amazon EBS Snapshots for data backup and recovery, and learned how they help protect data from unexpected failures.

## Day 4 - 7/5: Comparing Amazon EBS Volume Types

**Work completed:** I studied the storage options provided by Amazon EBS, including General Purpose SSD (gp3), Provisioned IOPS SSD, Throughput Optimized HDD, and Cold HDD. I compared their performance, pricing, and recommended use cases to understand how each storage type meets different application requirements.

**Result and lesson:** I learned the differences between SSD- and HDD-based storage on AWS and understood how to select the most suitable storage option to balance performance and cost.

## Day 5 - 8/5: Weekly Review and Knowledge Consolidation

**Work completed:** I reviewed all the topics covered during the week, including Amazon EBS, EBS volumes, snapshots, and the various SSD and HDD storage options. I also cleaned up the AWS resources created during the practice sessions and organized my study notes for future reference and internship documentation.

**Result and lesson:** By the end of the third week, I had developed a solid understanding of storage management on AWS, including how to back up data using snapshots and choose the appropriate storage solution for different deployment scenarios.
