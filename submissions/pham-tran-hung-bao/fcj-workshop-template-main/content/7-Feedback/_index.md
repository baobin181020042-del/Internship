This section contains my personal reflection after joining the **First Cloud AI Journey** program and completing the IRMS internship project. Overall, it was a valuable experience because I not only learned AWS services, but also practiced teamwork, documentation, and project delivery.

## 1. Learning and Internship Environment

The internship environment was open and suitable for students who are still learning and experimenting. I had room to explore, make mistakes, fix them, and learn from the process. I especially appreciated that the program encouraged not only technical completion but also documentation through the Worklog, Proposal, and Workshop.

One possible improvement is to have more periodic small-group reviews. Earlier reviews could help students detect problems in documentation, website structure, or presentation before too much work accumulates near the end.

## 2. Support from Mentor and Team

Mentors and support members helped guide me when I encountered AWS issues. Instead of simply giving answers, they often suggested what to check, such as logs, regions, permissions, or resource names. This helped me remember issues better and understand the root cause more clearly.

I also learned from my teammates. Since IRMS was a five-member team project, each person handled a different scope, so regular communication was necessary. I learned that a complete product requires more than working code; it also needs stable integration, clear documentation, and reasonable responsibility sharing.

## 3. Work Content and Knowledge Gained

The internship tasks matched my interest in cloud, backend, and infrastructure. Through IRMS, I worked with AWS services such as Cognito, API Gateway, Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, EventBridge, SNS, CloudFront, and AWS SAM. This gave me more practical experience than only reading theory.

Besides technical skills, I improved my reporting and documentation skills. Turning personal notes into Worklog and Workshop content taught me to write more clearly, organize content better, and avoid exposing sensitive information in documentation.

## 4. Suggestions

- Provide a checklist template for each report section so students can review their work before submission.
- Add a short session on writing Worklogs and Workshops in a professional style instead of a personal diary style.
- Encourage cross-review between teams because an outside reader can easily find missing or unclear parts.
- Provide examples of how to describe individual contribution in a team project.

## 5. Overall Reflection

After the internship, I feel more confident working with AWS and better understand how a cloud project is delivered from beginning to end. Although I still have many things to improve, this experience gave me a strong foundation for cloud engineering, backend/serverless development, and technical documentation.
